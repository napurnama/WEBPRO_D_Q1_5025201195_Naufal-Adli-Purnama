Magelang

Kota Sejuta Bunga

Magelang City’s reputation as a “green city” dates since before Indonesia’s independence. Since the Dutch Colonization, Magelang has been known as “Tuin Van Java”, which means “Java’s Garden”. Local government programs have aimed to enhance Magelang’s “greenness”, such as the planting of Tabebuya trees near Pemkot Magelang.

Kota Tertua Kedua

Magelang is Indonesia’s second oldest city, established on the 11th of April 907, then known as Mantyasih. Now, more than 120.000 call Magelang their home.
